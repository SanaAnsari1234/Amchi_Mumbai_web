Amchi Mumbai

City of Dream's

Mumbai is a vibrant metropolis known for its bustling markets, iconic landmarks, delicious street food, and rich cultural heritage. It's a major economic and entertainment hub, often called the "City of Dreams" The cosmopolitan vibe of Mumbai deftly balances its global mind-set with a proud nod to local culture. And the shopping haunts in the city are no different. Explore the homegrown labels that populate its many by-lanes in these key neighbourhoods below.

By Sana Ansari -Wednesday 23 July, 2025

Churchgate:

Seaside Churchgate is known for sports grounds like the vast Oval Maidan, popular for casual games of cricket, and nearby 1930s Brabourne Stadium, which hosts formal matches.
The area’s neo-Gothic and art deco buildings house offices and colleges, while restaurants range from fine Indian dining to old-world bistros serving evening tea and pastries.
Crowded Churchgate railway station is a hub for suburban trains. Start at Fabindia – its artisanal mix of clothing, bed linen, spices and beauty products is ideal for gifts. Saroj Fabrics and Niharikaa Fabrics (+91 22 2648 5544) on Khar Danda Road are both treasure troves of fabrics, selling everything from printed cottons to handwoven Benarasis. Bargain hunters will be drawn to Hill Road’s street shopping, while fans of luxury brands won’t be disappointed by multi-designer boutiques Atosa (+91 22 2640 1029) and Ensemble. For personalised bejewelled pieces, take a trip to Monisha Jaising.

Don’t miss: The hand-embroidered chinkankari tunics and kaftans at Neemrana.

Mumbai Central:

For a taste of local culture, visit Colaba Causeway for shopping and Haji Ali Dargah, a shrine on the Arabian Sea. To experience nature, head to Sanjay Gandhi National Park or Juhu Beach.
Shree Siddhivinayak Temple: This is one of Mumbai's most revered Hindu temples and a major tourist attraction.
Colaba Causeway: Bargain for souvenirs, handicrafts, and artifacts at this bustling market.
Bandra-Worli Sea Link: Witness this architectural marvel and enjoy panoramic views of the city skyline. Nehru Planetarium and Science Centre: Engage in some educational fun and learn about science and astronomy. Global Vipassana Pagoda: Visit this serene Buddhist monument, a symbol of peace and harmony.

Don’t miss: Gateway of India, Marine Drive, Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus, Shree Siddhivinayak Temple, and Elephanta Caves.

Bandra:

Wearing its hipster cred like a badge of honour, Bandra’s leafy lanes are full of understated local stores and luxe Indian designers. Venture beyond Linking Road for insider gems the locals swear by.
Start at Fabindia – its artisanal mix of clothing, bed linen, spices and beauty products is ideal for gifts. Saroj Fabrics and Niharikaa Fabrics (+91 22 2648 5544) on Khar Danda Road are both treasure troves of fabrics, selling everything from printed cottons to handwoven Benarasis. Bargain hunters will be drawn to Hill Road’s street shopping, while fans of luxury brands won’t be disappointed by multi-designer boutiques Atosa (+91 22 2640 1029) and Ensemble. For personalised bejewelled pieces, take a trip to Monisha Jaising

Don’t miss: The coastal location offers scenic views and leisure activities.

Lower Parel:

Once a collection of industrial mills, today you’ll find restaurants, bars, shops and offices, creating the area’s characteristic bustle, whatever time of day you visit.
Make a beeline for Raghuvanshi Mills, which is populated with some of the city’s best homeware stores, including Oma, Address Home (+91 22 4004 0474) and Pinakin. That box ticked, prepare to lose hours to Good Earth a sustainable luxury design brand steeped in Indian heritage.

Elsewhere, in Palladium Mall, stop by shops such as Anita Dongre, Masaba, Ritu Kumar and AM:PM, which are all go-tos for your fashion fix. Stock up on traditional handcrafted juttis for yourself and friends back home at Fizzy Goblet.

Don’t miss: Whether you’re a beauty connoisseur or not, making pit stop at organic Ayurvedic brand Forest Essentials is non-negotiable.

Andheri:

One of the most sought-after residential areas in Mumbai, Andheri West is a popular suburb with a lot of commercial and retail development.
Home to many big production houses, it is located in close proximity to important social and commercial hubs like BKC, Lower Parel, SEEPZ, MIDC etc. It is well-connected with the rest of the city via the western railway network, roads, transport facilities, with close proximity to both the domestic and international airports.
It's home to prominent hospitals like Holy Spirit OPD, Kokilaben Dhirubhai Ambani Hospital, Seven Hills, educational institutes like SP Jain, Bhavan's College, Ryan Global School, shopping malls like the Infinity Mall, City Mall, cafes, restaurants, multiplexes, public spaces and recreation centers and so on. Development of social infrastructure and urban amenities has contributed to the huge demand for real estate here making it an important realty hub.

Don’t miss: the vibrant Lokhandwala Complex Market, the historic Mahakali Caves, and the beautiful Versova Beach

Borivali:

Borivali lies at the northern tip of western Mumbai . Its one of the major suburbs of Mumbai ; social amenities include hospitals, schools, colleges, lovely cafes, good restaurants, shopping malls. Borivali is famed as the "suburb of gardens" — its a lovely green suburb with gardens at almost every block.
Borivali in Mumbai is special for its natural beauty and historical sites. The Sanjay Gandhi National Park offers a haven of greenery and wildlife, while the Kanheri Caves provide glimpses into ancient Buddhist history. The Global Vipassana Pagoda stands out as a prominent architectural landmark and a center for meditation
Borivali has emerged as one of the most desirable residential areas in Mumbai, offering a perfect mix of modern infrastructure, excellent connectivity, and a high quality of life.

Don’t miss: the vibrant Sanjay Gandhi National Park, the historical Kanheri Caves, he Global Vipassana Pagoda, the Fish Park, known for its diverse fish species, and the peaceful Gorai Beach

Vasai:

Vasai-Virar City is the only Metropolitan City (having population more than 10 lakhs in Palghar district which is also having Municipal Corporation. It is located at North Mumbai.
Vasai is famous for its historical Bassein Fort, which served as a naval base and shipbuilding center for the Portuguese. It's also known for its beaches, like Bhuigaon Beach and Rangaon Beach, as well as the Tungareshwar Temple and the Tungareshwar Wildlife Sanctuary.
Vasai is a historical suburban town north of Mumbai and it is located in Palghar district. It is a much modern part of Vasai Taluka. It is a part of the new Vasai-Virar city.

Don’t miss: exploring the Bassein Fort, a historic site with a rich past. Also, consider visiting Tungareshwar Temple for a unique trekking experience and wildlife at the Tungareshwar Wildlife Sanctuary. For nature lovers, Rangaon Beach and Bhuigaon Beach offer serene coastal views.

Virar:

Catering to people from all walks of life, Virar boasts of some very well-developed luxury resorts, waterparks as well as natural attractions like beaches and temples. Rajodi Beachside is one of the most visited outdoor spots that is highly rated by residents and visitors alike.
This is a great place to live, offering a balance between urban convenience and a peaceful environment. One of the best things about this place is its affordability, which makes it an ideal place to live. The connectivity to Mumbai via the Western Railway ensures easy commuting.

Don’t miss: Arnala Beach, the renowned Jivdani Temple, and the popular waterpark, Great Escape Water Park. Other attractions include Takmak Fort, Tungareshwar National Park, and Visava Waterpark & Beach Resort.
Early History:
Ancient Times: Mumbai was part of the Mauryan Empire in the 3rd century BCE.
Indigenous Communities: The Koli and Agri communities, speaking Marathi, were among the first permanent settlers around 600 BCE.
Portuguese Influence: In the 16th century, the Portuguese gained control of the islands and named them "Bom Bahia" (Good Harbor).
British Era: The British East India Company acquired the islands in the 17th century, leading to the city's growth as a major trading port and the capital of the Bombay Presidency.
